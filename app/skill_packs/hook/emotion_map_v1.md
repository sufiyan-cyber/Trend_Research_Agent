# Skill Pack: Emotion Map (v1)

This supersedes the short emotion list in the hook taxonomy. When naming the **dominant emotion**, pick exactly one term from this map, spelled as written — these are the canonical names the rest of the pipeline counts on. If none fits, name the emotion plainly in one lowercase word and accept it will be flagged as off-map.

## Positive — the state the campaign leaves the viewer in

joy · excitement · delight · amusement · love · trust · hope · optimism · inspiration · gratitude · relief · pride · confidence · comfort · satisfaction · curiosity · surprise · belonging

## Negative — the discomfort the campaign weaponizes

fear · anxiety · stress · anger · frustration · sadness · disappointment · disgust · shame · guilt · loneliness · confusion · boredom · jealousy · envy · regret · panic · doubt

Use the precise one: "everyone's ahead of you" is **anxiety** (not "insecurity"); comment-bait that picks a fight fires **anger**; "you wasted your college years" fires **regret**.

## Consumer-desire — wanting-states that convert

fomo · urgency · scarcity · exclusivity · luxury · status · achievement · empowerment · security · safety · convenience · freedom · adventure · nostalgia · anticipation · desire · aspiration · reward · self-improvement · control · escape · family · romance · health · social approval · value seeking · prestige · recognition

## Choosing between adjacent emotions

- **fomo vs anxiety** — fomo needs a *specific missable thing* (an event, a batch, a window). Generalized "you're falling behind" is anxiety.
- **aspiration vs envy** — aspiration says "that could be me"; envy says "why them and not me". Look at whether the viewer is invited into the outcome or merely shown it.
- **status vs pride** — status is wanting others to see it; pride is the internal feeling of having done it. Salary-screenshot posts are usually status.
- **urgency vs scarcity** — urgency is time running out; scarcity is supply running out. A countdown is urgency; "only 12 seats" is scarcity.
- **belonging vs social approval** — belonging is being *inside* the group; social approval is the group's applause. Community inside-jokes are belonging; "10,000 learners chose us" is social approval.

Name the **exact word or image** that fires the emotion — the trigger element is as important as the label, because it is what we transfer to our own audiences.
